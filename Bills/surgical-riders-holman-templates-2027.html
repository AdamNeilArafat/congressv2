Surgical Riders & Holman — (2027)

Tight riders and precise Holman language for use in CRs or minibuses. These fence publication, hosting, and staffing for non-compliant actions during covered periods.

Guidance

  • Keep riders narrow and functional. Avoid broad policy language.
  • Holman reductions must be clearly tied to retrenchment of funds and specific duties or positions.
  • Pair with committee report justifications to bolster House Rule XXI precedent.

Templates

Publication and Hosting Fence (Rider)

None of the funds made available by this Act may be used to publish in the Federal Register or the Code of Federal Regulations, print, transmit, host on official websites, or otherwise authenticate any executive document first initiated during a covered period unless the head of the issuing agency certifies compliance with the Implementation Safeguards reporting requirements or a life-safety exigency.

OIRA Intake Target (Holman)

None of the funds made available by this Act may be used to pay the salary of any employee of the Office of Information and Regulatory Affairs whose duties include clearing for publication any executive document first initiated during a covered period that lacks a valid OMB compliance identifier, except where the head of the issuing agency has certified a life-safety exigency.

Seals and Stationery Fence (Rider)

None of the funds in this Act may be used by the General Services Administration to furnish seals, stationery, or certificates authenticating any covered-period executive document lacking a valid OMB compliance identifier or life-safety certification.

IT Routing Block (Rider)

None of the funds in this Act may be used to deploy or host, on .gov systems, a covered-period executive document that lacks a valid OMB compliance identifier or life-safety certification.

Personnel Processing Hold (Rider)

None of the funds in this Act may be used by the Office of Personnel Management to onboard, transfer, or process personnel whose primary duties would implement a covered-period executive document lacking a valid OMB compliance identifier, absent a life-safety certification.
