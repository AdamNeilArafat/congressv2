Bill Blueprint

Impeachment Continuity and Accountability Act (2027)

Guardrails that keep government steady during impeachment: continuity of operations, clean spending rules, fast approval checks for new emergencies, records preservation, and communications integrity—enforced by GAO/IG oversight and expedited court review.

Continuity of Government Spending Lock & Certification Fast-Track Congressional Approval Records & Publication Integrity Whistleblower Protection Expedited Judicial Review

What this bill does

  • Continuity first: stabilizes chain-of-command, acting service rules, and mission-essential operations during an impeachment period.
  • Implementation safeguards: defines “funds” as money, supplies, staff time, IT, and facilities; requires written certifications and GAO/IG review before implementing major actions.
  • Emergency spending approval: for new emergency declarations during impeachment, requires a fast approval‑to‑obligate vote by Congress with narrow life‑safety carve‑outs.
  • Communications integrity: tightens Federal Register/GPO/OMB/OFR standards, record‑keeping, publication, and anti‑propaganda rules.
  • Real oversight: GAO audits, IG lanes, whistleblower shields, and expedited court timelines with injunctive relief.

Why it matters

Impeachment is rare—but when it happens, the stakes are enormous. This bill keeps the lights on, prevents last‑minute abuses, and ensures the public record stays honest and intact while Congress does its job.

How it’s paid for

  • Deficit‑neutral: primarily governance and oversight rules.
  • Targeted admin costs: authorizes modest funds for dashboards, audits, and secure archiving; offset via FSGG administrative savings and anti‑waste recoveries.

Safeguards

  • Life‑safety carve‑outs only for immediate protection and court‑ordered compliance.
  • Sunsets and reviews for emergency approval mechanisms.
  • Severability to preserve workable protections if any part is struck.

TITLE I — CONTINUITY OF GOVERNMENT & OPERATIONS

SEC. 101. DEFINITIONS; “IMPEACHMENT PERIOD”.

“Impeachment period” means the time from House adoption of articles of impeachment until final Senate verdict, dismissal, or expiration of 90 days without Senate action, whichever occurs first. “Major action” means: (1) new emergency declaration; (2) new executive order with substantial budgetary or legal effect; (3) rule designated “major” under 5 U.S.C. 804; (4) reprogramming/transfer above agency thresholds; (5) mass personnel actions of 100+ career employees or 20+ SES.

SEC. 102. CONTINUITY & ACTING SERVICE RULES.

Clarifies acting official designations; bars circumvention of Senate advice and consent via serial acting designations; maintains mission‑essential operations lists filed with OMB/OPM; prohibits structural reorganizations absent statutory authority.

SEC. 103. ESSENTIAL SERVICES PROTECTION.

Requires continuity plans for public safety, veterans’ care, Social Security/Medicare operations, critical infrastructure, and national security support; GAO/IG readiness spot‑checks.

TITLE II — IMPLEMENTATION SAFEGUARDS DURING IMPEACHMENT

SEC. 201. “FUNDS” DEFINED BROADLY.

For this title, “funds” include appropriated dollars, supplies, staff time, IT services, and use of Federal facilities. Explanation: Stops end‑runs where implementation proceeds using non‑cash resources.

SEC. 202. CERTIFICATIONS, GAO/IG LANES, & EXPEDITED COURT REVIEW.

Before implementing any major action during an impeachment period, the head of the agency must certify legal authority, necessity, and cost impacts; transmit to GAO, relevant IG, OMB, and Congress; actions become challengeable on an expedited schedule in the D.C. Circuit; courts may grant injunctive relief.

SEC. 203. SUSPENSION OF MAJOR RULE IMPLEMENTATION ABSENT CERTIFICATION.

Major rules published during an impeachment period are stayed unless certifications under §202 are complete; life‑safety and court‑ordered compliance exempted.

TITLE III — EMERGENCY POWERS APPROVAL‑TO‑OBLIGATE

SEC. 301. APPROVAL REQUIREMENT.

No funds may be obligated or expended to implement a new emergency declaration first issued during an impeachment period unless a joint resolution of approval is enacted within 7 legislative days after notice to Congress.

SEC. 302. CARVE‑OUTS.

Immediate force‑protection, life‑saving actions, disaster response already underway, and court‑ordered compliance may proceed for up to 7 days contingent on written certifications; lapse absent approval.

SEC. 303. FAST‑TRACK PROCEDURES.

Any Member may introduce an approval resolution; privileged status; limited debate; no dilatory motions; guaranteed up‑or‑down floor vote in each chamber within 7 legislative days.

SEC. 304. PILOT & SUNSET.

Pilot applies government‑wide for 2 years after enactment; report due at 18 months on effectiveness and recommended permanent standards.

TITLE IV — COMMUNICATIONS & PUBLICATION INTEGRITY

SEC. 401. FEDERAL REGISTER & GPO TIMELINES.

OFR and GPO shall publish executive actions promptly; improper delays are reportable to GAO; agencies must provide machine‑readable texts, authority citations, and cost statements.

SEC. 402. RECORDS PRESERVATION & DISCLOSURE.

Mandates preservation of Presidential and agency records (including electronic messages and encrypted channels) related to any major action; establishes a litigation hold during the impeachment period; accelerates FOIA processing for covered materials.

SEC. 403. OLC/OMB/OIRA TRANSPARENCY INDEX.

Requires an indexed, non‑privileged summary log of controlling OLC opinions and OIRA determinations relied upon for major actions, with dates, authorities, and scope; classified annex permitted.

TITLE V — OVERSIGHT, ENFORCEMENT & WHISTLEBLOWERS

SEC. 501. GAO & IG OVERSIGHT.

GAO conducts quarterly audits of certifications, emergency approvals, and publication compliance; IGs receive dedicated hotlines and may issue immediate compliance directives.

SEC. 502. BENEFICIARY & CONGRESSIONAL ENFORCEMENT.

Authorizes suits by affected parties and committees to enjoin violations of Titles II–IV; exclusive venue in D.C. District Court; 14‑day briefing; 7‑day decision target; direct appeal to D.C. Circuit.

SEC. 503. WHISTLEBLOWER & IG SHIELDS.

Strengthens OSC timelines; expands anti‑retaliation protections for disclosures related to covered actions; bars removal of IGs without cause during an impeachment period.

TITLE VI — IMPLEMENTATION, BUDGET, SEVERABILITY

SEC. 601. EFFECTIVE DATE.

Effective on enactment; Titles II–IV apply prospectively to actions taken during an impeachment period beginning after enactment.

SEC. 602. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

Authorizes such sums as necessary (FY27–FY29) for GAO/IG surge, secure archiving, and dashboard publication; offsets from FSGG administrative savings and anti‑waste recoveries.

SEC. 603. SEVERABILITY; SUNSET REVIEW.

Severability applies to all titles; emergency approval pilot sunsets per §304 absent reauthorization; GAO submits a comprehensive review at 24 months with recommendations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this block disaster response?

No. Life‑safety, active disaster response, and court‑ordered compliance are carved out with short, renewable certifications.

Is this a power grab by Congress?

It’s a time‑limited balance: Congress confirms new emergency spending during impeachment while day‑to‑day government continues.

Does this freeze all regulations?

No. It pauses major actions without basic certifications and transparency; routine and safety‑critical actions continue.

What stops end‑runs through “acting” officials?

Serial acting appointments and structural reorganizations are restricted; GAO and IGs can flag violations for rapid court review.

Bill Text

A BILL

To safeguard continuity of government and public accountability during impeachment; to condition new emergency spending on rapid congressional approval; to require certifications and oversight for major actions; to strengthen records and publication integrity; and for other purposes.

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
This Act may be cited as the “Impeachment Continuity and Accountability Act”.

SECTION 2. FINDINGS AND PURPOSES.
(a) Findings.—Congress finds that impeachment periods present heightened risks of disorder, record loss, and hasty actions; that continuity must be preserved; and that transparent, time-limited approval and certification mechanisms protect the public interest.
(b) Purposes.—To ensure continuity of government; prevent abuses of emergency authorities; protect records and publication integrity; and provide enforceable oversight with expedited judicial review.

TITLE I—CONTINUITY OF GOVERNMENT & OPERATIONS
Sec. 101. Definitions; “impeachment period”.
Sec. 102. Acting service rules; prohibited circumvention.
Sec. 103. Essential services protection.

TITLE II—IMPLEMENTATION SAFEGUARDS
Sec. 201. “Funds” include money, supplies, staff time, IT, and facilities.
Sec. 202. Certifications; GAO/IG review; expedited judicial review.
Sec. 203. Stay of major rules absent certification; life-safety exceptions.

TITLE III—EMERGENCY POWERS APPROVAL-TO-OBLIGATE
Sec. 301. No obligation of funds for new emergency declarations absent a joint resolution within 7 legislative days.
Sec. 302. Life-safety and court-order carve-outs with 7-day renewable certifications.
Sec. 303. Fast-track procedures; privileged consideration; guaranteed floor votes.
Sec. 304. 2-year pilot; GAO report; sunset unless reauthorized.

TITLE IV—COMMUNICATIONS & PUBLICATION INTEGRITY
Sec. 401. Federal Register and GPO publication timelines; authority and cost statements.
Sec. 402. Records preservation; litigation holds; accelerated FOIA processing.
Sec. 403. Transparency index for controlling OLC/OIRA determinations.

TITLE V—OVERSIGHT, ENFORCEMENT & WHISTLEBLOWERS
Sec. 501. GAO audits; IG hotlines; compliance directives.
Sec. 502. Beneficiary and congressional enforcement; expedited venue and appeal.
Sec. 503. Whistleblower protections; bar on IG removals without cause during impeachment periods.

TITLE VI—IMPLEMENTATION, BUDGET, SEVERABILITY
Sec. 601. Effective date; prospective application.
Sec. 602. Authorization of appropriations; offsets.
Sec. 603. Severability; 24-month review and recommendations.
